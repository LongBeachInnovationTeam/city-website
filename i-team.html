Meet the Long Beach i-team

The City of Long Beach has assembled a team of local, regional and national talent with a unique and comprehensive set of skills to advance its innovation projects. The team includes a full-time cultural anthropologist, two industrial designers, a software developer, and three innovation fellows.

Alma Castro is one of our Innovation Fellows/Project Managers. She completed her Master’s Degree in Public Administration in 2011 from the Robert F. Wagner School of Public Service at New York University. Her background is in program management, curriculum development, and evaluation. She currently lives in Long Beach.

Alex Chavez is our team’s software developer who uses his experience and skills in technology to make his hometown a more connected place. Graduated locally from Cabrillo High School and from California State University, Long Beach with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Computer Science.

Harrison Huynh is the team’s designer completed his bachelor’s degree in Industrial Design at CSU, Long Beach. In 2007 he won an ‘Innovations Award’ for his design work at the Consumer Electronic Show (CES) in Las Vegas. His designs and concepts were eventually released into big box retail. In 2009 Harrison joined Solar Monkey as a Designer, to help give direction and build the overall brand of the company.

John Keisler is the Director of the Innovation Team and has lived in Long Beach for the past 12 years. He has a Masters in Public Administration from the USC Price School of Public Policy. Over the years, he’s led multiple change-management projects throughout the City, serving as the Animal Care Services Bureau Manager, Business Operations Manager for the Parks, Recreation & Marine Department, and CFO for the Police Department.

Eric Romero is one of our Innovation Fellows/Project Managers, he holds a Master’s in Urban and Regional Planning from UCLA, with a focus in economic development. Eric most recently worked as a Project Manager for Global Public Affairs at UCLA Luskin, where he oversaw program operations and curriculum development. He has previously worked in the nonprofit sector around issues of economic empowerment and immigrant integration.

Heidi Wiersma is one of our Innovation Fellows/ Project Managers holds an MPA from USC and a BS in Community Health from Western Washington University. Prior to working for the City she worked in public health and served in the Peace Corps. Both experiences have affirmed her belief in the power of local government to protect and enhance lives through sound policy practices. She currently lives in Long Beach.

Meet the Long Beach i-team

The City of Long Beach has assembled a team of local, regional and national talent with a unique and comprehensive set of skills to advance its innovation projects. The team includes a full-time cultural anthropologist, two industrial designers, a software developer, and three innovation fellows.

Alex Chavez is our team’s software developer who uses his experience and skills in technology to make his hometown a more connected place. Graduated locally from Cabrillo High School and from California State University, Long Beach with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Computer Science.

Alma Castro is one of our Innovation Fellows/Project Managers. She completed her Master’s Degree in Public Administration in 2011 from the Robert F. Wagner School of Public Service at New York University. Her background is in program management, curriculum development, and evaluation. She currently lives in Long Beach.

Eric Romero is one of our Innovation Fellows/Project Managers, he holds a Master’s in Urban and Regional Planning from UCLA, with a focus in economic development. Eric most recently worked as a Project Manager for Global Public Affairs at UCLA Luskin, where he oversaw program operations and curriculum development. He has previously worked in the nonprofit sector around issues of economic empowerment and immigrant integration.

Harrison Huynh is the team’s designer completed his bachelor’s degree in Industrial Design at CSU, Long Beach. In 2007 he won an ‘Innovations Award’ for his design work at the Consumer Electronic Show (CES) in Las Vegas. His designs and concepts were eventually released into big box retail. In 2009 Harrison joined Solar Monkey as a Designer, to help give direction and build the overall brand of the company.

Heidi Wiersma is one of our Innovation Fellows/ Project Managers holds an MPA from USC and a BS in Community Health from Western Washington University. Prior to working for the City she worked in public health and served in the Peace Corps. Both experiences have affirmed her belief in the power of local government to protect and enhance lives through sound policy practices. She currently lives in Long Beach.

Holly Okonkwo is a mixed-methods cultural anthropologist who specializes in issues of diversity and inclusion in Science and Technology in the United States. Her areas of expertise are Science and Technology Policy, Education, diversity and inclusion. Dr. Okonkwo completed a PhD in Anthropology at the University of California, Riverside. She also holds a Master's in Education Policy and Leadership.

John Keisler is the Director of the Innovation Team and has lived in Long Beach for the past 12 years. He has a Masters in Public Administration from the USC Price School of Public Policy. Over the years, he’s led multiple change-management projects throughout the City, serving as the Animal Care Services Bureau Manager, Business Operations Manager for the Parks, Recreation & Marine Department, and CFO for the Police Department.

Justine Esquivel is a Designmatters Fellow and current graduate student in the Media Design Practices program at the Art Center College of Design where she is focusing on interaction design in urban spaces. Originally from Seattle, she has a BFA in Cultural Communication and Spanish from the University of Washington. Her previous work in research, business development, and multimedia storytelling has allowed her to work and live in places such as Rwanda and Buenos Aires. She sees the city as a place for its residents to thrive and have a voice in its development and the power of design to facilitate that process.

Ryan Murray is the Mayor’s Innovation Deputy and holds a Masters Degree in Public Health from Hunter College, City University of New York. Ryan serves on the Mayor’s senior staff and functions as an advisor and strategist for the i-team. Prior to this role, for fifteen years he served in a variety of executive positions in nonprofit organizations and New York City government, and focused on opportunity creation for those most vulnerable.

Previous i-team Designmatters Fellows

Brittany Banayan is a Designmatters Fellow and a recent graduate of Art Center College of Design with a BFA in Graphic Design. With an emphasis in brand strategy and design thinking, she is passionate about collaborating with a multi-disciplinary team to implement innovative policy solutions to achieve meaningful impact.

Chase Airmet, our first Designmatters fellow is currently a graduate Industrial Design candidate at the Art Center College of Design. He has a drive and passion to create objects, services and experiences that excite and delight users. Chase seeks to design new growth and value for companies through a trend and research based iterative design process.

Karen Davison is a Designmatters Fellow and is currently a Graphic Design student at Art Center College of Design. After graduating from Art Center with her first BFA in Illustration in 2006, she returned in 2014 to pursue her love of design. Karen's goal is to use her unique understanding of art and design to solve real-world problems and work on projects that meaningfully improve the quality of life for the people who live in her community.
